\subsection{Sud Aviation Alouette III}

The Sud Aviation (later AÃ©rospatiale) Alouette III is a scout, utility, and light attack helicopter. It can carry a pilot and seven passengers. It can be armed with twin 0.303-in Browning machine guns, a single 7.62 mm MAG machine gun, or a 20 mm Matra MG 151 cannon firing out of the left door. Alternatively, it can be armed with \binarymultiply{7}{68 mm} RPs, four SS.11 RGs, two AS.12 RGs, or two torpedos.

The Alouette III entered service in France in 1960 and was widely exported. It served in the Rhodesian and South African Bush Wars, where versions with LMG door guns were known as G-Cars and those with 20 mm door guns as K-Cars.
