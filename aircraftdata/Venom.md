## Notes and Changes

### Venom FB.1

- Originally referred to as the Vampire FB.8. Interim aircraft until the Hunter and Swifts could be developed (Watkins).
- Vampire fuselage and tail with new wings with a slightly swept leading edge and more powerful Ghost engine.
- Poor roll rate was "poor without wing-tip tanks and deplorable with them" (Watkins)
- Instabilities at high Mach-number (Watkins). Pitch-up at Mach 0.86 = 640 mph at sea level.
- Ineffective air brakes
- Control buzz above 400 mph
- Goblin 4850 lb (Watkins)
- "climbs at an exceptional rate" (Watkins)

- Eight RP-3s (Watkins)
- Wing-tip FTs are 78 gal = 350L = 115 fuel points (Watkins)
- Two 500 lb or two 1000 lb bomb (Watkins)
- 342 gal fuel (Watkins).

- Able to reach 50k ft and 52k ft when striped (Watkins)

- Licence-built in Switzerland (Watkins)

- RAF (Watkins). 
  - Operational in Germany from August 1952 until late 1957 (Watkins).
  - Operational in MEAF from February 1954 (Watkins)
    - Action in Aden 1955 to 1960 (Watkins)
    - Action in Oman 1957 to 1959 (Watkins)
    - Action in Suez 1956 (Watkins)
- Switzerland (Watkins)
