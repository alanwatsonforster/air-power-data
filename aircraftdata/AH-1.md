# AH-1


Fuselage length in 44 ft

## AH-1G

- TAT-102A with M134 with 8000 rounds (80 shots of ammo)
- TAT-141 with two M134 and 4000 rounds (20 shots of ammo)
- Could substitute one M134 for a M129 grenade launcher
- In combat in Vietnam from 1967
- Four weapon pylones
  - Four 7 x and 19 x 70 mm RPs. I've not seen any with four 19 x 70 RPs, but many have a pair of each.
  - Two SU-11/A minigun pods with 1500 rounds
  - From 1969, one M35 GP with a 20 mm M195 (M61A1) with 950 rounds on left inner pylon. 950 rounds at 6000 RPM is 5 shots.
- IRM countermeasures (modified exhaust and AN/ALQ-144 IRCM unit) from 1972

## AH-1J (International)

- Twin-engined
- 8 TOW
- Iran and South Koorea

## AH-1Q

- AH-1G conversion
- Deliveries from 1975
- 8 TOWs in two four packs on the outer pylines.
- Under powered

## AH-1S

- Up-engined AH-1Q
- Deliveries complete in 1979
- Also known as "Improved AH-1S", "AH-1S Modified", or "AH-1S(MOD)" prior to 1988
- Cannot carry RPs
- Exhaust deflector

## AH-1P

- AH-1S(PROD) before 1988
- Delivered 1977 and 1978
- Flat glass
- Cannot carry RPs
- RWR

## AH-1E

- AH-1S(ECAS) before 1988
- Delivered 1978 and 1979
- M197 cannon
- RP capability
- HMS

## AH-1F

- All improvements in AH-1P and AH-1E
- AN/ALQ-144 IRCM and long exhaust
- Delivered 1979 to 1986
- Served until 1999 in Army and 2001 in NG

## AH-1J

- Twin-engined
- USMC in service 1970
- M197 20 mm with 750 RPM and 750 rounds = 30 shots

## AH-1T

- Twin-engined based on AH-1J
- TOW capable and later Hellfire
- USMC in late 1970s

## AH-1W

- Twin-engined based on AH-1T
- USMC from 1986
- AN/ALQ-144 IRCM and long exhaust
- AN/AAR-47 missile warning
- AN/ALE-39 chaff-flare dispensers
- AN/APR-39A(V)2 radar warning receiver 
- CCD TV imager, a FLIR, and a laser target designator with automatic target tracking capabilities
- AIM-9L, AGM122

## AH-1Z

- Twin-engined based on AH-1W
- USMC in 2011
- Six stations. Wingtips can carry Sidewiunder or Longbow on right wingtip.
- four AN/ALE-47 chaff-flare dispensers
- AN/ALQ-144 IRCM was not fitted
- exhaust system also had a very low infrared signature.

## AN/ALE-39(V) RWR

- Installed on J and T according to 1986 manual
- Can detect:
  - SA-2 search and track (both LF)
  - SA-3 search and track
  - SA-4 search and track (LF and HF)
  - SA-6 search and track (MF and HF)
  - SA-8 search and track (HF and VF)
  - ZSU-23-4 search and track (VF)
  - S-60 search and track (LF)
- The ability to detect VF FCR and VF TTR suggest this is RWR-C

## AN/ALE-44

- Installed on J and T according to 1986 manual
- Can detect CW
- This capability is present in RWR-C

## AN/AVR-2

- Installed on J and T according to 1986 manual
- Detects laser designation

## AN/ALE-39

- Installed on J and T according to 1986 manual
- Can dispense FL, CH, and MJ
- Programmable in flight
- Two units each with 30 decoys
- This seems to be a DDS-C

## AN/ALQ-144

- Installed on J and T according to 1986 manual
- IR jammer
- Omnidirectional
